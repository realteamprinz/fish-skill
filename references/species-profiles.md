# Fish Species Profile Reference

## How Species Data Is Used

Fish species have wildly different care requirements, temperaments, and lifespans. Species data is used to:
1. Set baseline expectations for behavior and compatibility
2. Flag species-specific health concerns
3. Ensure proper water parameters are maintained
4. Predict social dynamics in community tanks

## Common Freshwater Species

### Betta (Siamese Fighting Fish)
- Temperament: Males aggressive to other males, variable with tankmates
- Swimming Level: Top to mid
- Temperature: 76-82°F (24-28°C)
- pH: 6.5-7.5
- Tank Size: 5 gallons minimum (despite marketing)
- Lifespan: 3-5 years
- Notable: Labyrinth breather (gulps air at surface), incredible fin diversity
- Health Watch: Fin rot (very common), velvet, swim bladder issues

### Neon Tetra
- Temperament: Peaceful, schooling (6+ minimum)
- Swimming Level: Mid
- Temperature: 70-81°F (21-27°C)
- pH: 6.0-7.0
- Tank Size: 10 gallons minimum for a school
- Lifespan: 5-10 years (longer than most expect)
- Notable: Tight schooling when stressed, loose when comfortable
- Health Watch: Neon tetra disease (incurable, contagious), ich

### Corydoras Catfish
- Temperament: Peaceful, social (4+ minimum)
- Swimming Level: Bottom
- Temperature: 72-79°F (22-26°C)
- pH: 6.0-7.5
- Tank Size: 20 gallons for a group
- Lifespan: 5-10 years
- Notable: Must have sand or smooth substrate (barbels are delicate), adorable "winking"
- Health Watch: Barbel erosion from sharp substrate, ich

### African Cichlids
- Temperament: Aggressive, territorial, intelligent
- Swimming Level: All levels depending on species
- Temperature: 76-82°F (24-28°C)
- pH: 7.8-8.6 (hard, alkaline water)
- Tank Size: 55+ gallons
- Lifespan: 5-15 years depending on species
- Notable: Rock-dwelling vs open-water species, mouth-brooding, recognize their owners
- Health Watch: Malawi bloat, aggression injuries

### Goldfish
- Temperament: Peaceful, social
- Swimming Level: All levels
- Temperature: 65-72°F (18-22°C) — COLDWATER, not tropical
- pH: 7.0-8.4
- Tank Size: 20 gallons for first, 10 per additional (fancy), pond for commons
- Lifespan: 10-25 years (yes, really)
- Notable: Massive waste producers, need powerful filtration, grow much larger than expected
- Health Watch: Swim bladder issues (especially fancy), ich, fin rot

### Plecostomus
- Temperament: Peaceful, nocturnal
- Swimming Level: Bottom
- Temperature: 72-82°F (22-28°C)
- pH: 6.5-7.5
- Tank Size: Varies hugely by species (bristlenose: 20gal, common pleco: 100+ gal)
- Lifespan: 10-20 years
- Notable: Common plecos grow to 12-24 inches — most tanks are too small
- Health Watch: Starvation (they need more than just algae), wood for digestion

### Guppy
- Temperament: Peaceful, active
- Swimming Level: Top to mid
- Temperature: 72-82°F (22-28°C)
- pH: 7.0-8.0
- Tank Size: 10 gallons
- Lifespan: 2-3 years
- Notable: Prolific breeders — will overpopulate if males and females together
- Health Watch: Guppy disease, fin nipping from other species

## Marine Species

### Clownfish
- Temperament: Semi-aggressive, territorial around their anemone
- Temperature: 75-82°F (24-28°C)
- Salinity: 1.020-1.025 specific gravity
- Tank Size: 20 gallons minimum
- Lifespan: 6-10 years in captivity
- Notable: Form symbiotic relationships with anemones, all born male (dominant becomes female)

### Tang
- Temperament: Semi-aggressive, active swimmers
- Temperature: 75-82°F (24-28°C)
- Tank Size: 75-180+ gallons depending on species (they need swimming room)
- Lifespan: 8-20 years
- Notable: Important algae grazers in reef tanks, prone to ich
- Health Watch: Ich/marine white spot (very susceptible), HLLE
