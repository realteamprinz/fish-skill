# Water Chemistry Reference

## The Big Five Parameters

### 1. Temperature
- Most tropical fish: 75-80°F (24-27°C)
- Goldfish: 65-72°F (18-22°C)
- Stability matters MORE than exact number
- Rapid temperature swings (>2°F in an hour) cause stress and ich outbreaks
- Heater failure is one of the most common fish killers

### 2. pH
- Measures acidity/alkalinity (7.0 = neutral)
- Most freshwater fish: 6.5-7.5
- African cichlids: 7.8-8.6
- Stability is more important than chasing a "perfect" number
- pH changes are logarithmic — 6.0 is 10x more acidic than 7.0
- Never chase pH with chemicals — crashes are worse than imperfect pH

### 3. Ammonia (NH3/NH4+)
- MUST be 0 in a cycled tank
- Any reading above 0 = action needed
- More toxic at higher pH and temperature
- Sources: fish waste, uneaten food, dead organisms, tap water (chloramine)
- Emergency treatment: large water change, Prime/detoxifier

### 4. Nitrite (NO2-)
- MUST be 0 in a cycled tank
- Any reading above 0 = action needed
- Prevents fish from absorbing oxygen (brown blood disease)
- Emergency treatment: large water change, salt (0.1-0.3%)

### 5. Nitrate (NO3-)
- Keep below 40 ppm (below 20 is better)
- Only removed by water changes or plants
- Gradually rising nitrate is normal between water changes
- Sudden jump = something died or filter issue

## Secondary Parameters

### GH (General Hardness)
- Measures calcium and magnesium
- Affects fish osmoregulation
- Soft water fish (tetras, rasboras): 3-8 dGH
- Hard water fish (cichlids, livebearers): 10-20 dGH
- Shrimp often need specific GH for molting

### KH (Carbonate Hardness)
- Buffers pH — prevents crashes
- Low KH = pH can drop suddenly (dangerous)
- Minimum 2-3 dKH for stability
- Higher KH = more stable pH

## Water Changes

### Why
- Remove nitrate
- Replenish minerals
- Dilute dissolved organic compounds
- Reset water chemistry drift

### How Much and How Often
- General guideline: 25-50% weekly
- Heavily stocked: may need more frequent
- Lightly stocked with plants: may need less
- Consistency matters — same day each week is ideal
- Always match temperature when adding new water

### Dechlorination
- Tap water contains chlorine or chloramine — both kill beneficial bacteria and fish
- ALWAYS use a dechlorinator (Prime, Safe, etc.)
- Chloramine = chlorine + ammonia — dechlorinators detoxify both components
- Never skip this step
