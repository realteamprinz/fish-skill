# Tank Ecosystem Reference

## The Nitrogen Cycle

The most critical concept in fishkeeping. Understanding where your tank is in this cycle saves fish lives.

### The Cycle
1. Fish produce waste (ammonia) — TOXIC
2. Beneficial bacteria convert ammonia to nitrite — TOXIC
3. Different bacteria convert nitrite to nitrate — tolerable at low levels
4. Water changes remove nitrate

### Cycling Status Indicators
- **Uncycled:** Ammonia and/or nitrite present — DANGEROUS
- **Cycling:** Ammonia dropping, nitrite appearing — IN PROGRESS
- **Cycled:** 0 ammonia, 0 nitrite, some nitrate — SAFE
- **Mini-cycle:** Temporary spike after filter cleaning, medication, or major changes

### When Things Go Wrong
- Ammonia spike → Check for: dead fish, overfeeding, filter failure, overstocking
- Nitrite spike → Check for: disrupted bacteria (new filter media, medication)
- Nitrate climbing fast → Check for: overstocking, overfeeding, insufficient water changes

## Water Parameters

### Critical Parameters to Track
| Parameter | Freshwater Range | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature | Species-dependent | Affects metabolism, oxygen, disease |
| pH | 6.0-8.5 (species-dependent) | Affects toxicity of ammonia, fish stress |
| Ammonia (NH3/NH4) | 0 ppm (always) | Toxic at any level, burns gills |
| Nitrite (NO2) | 0 ppm (always) | Prevents oxygen uptake |
| Nitrate (NO3) | <40 ppm (<20 ideal) | Stress and health issues at high levels |
| GH (General Hardness) | Species-dependent | Affects osmoregulation |
| KH (Carbonate Hardness) | 4-8 dKH typical | Buffers pH — low KH = pH crashes |

### Parameter Logging
Track parameters over time to:
- Establish baselines for YOUR tank
- Detect trends before they become problems
- Correlate parameter changes with fish behavior
- Optimize water change frequency

## Filtration

### Types
- **Hang-On-Back (HOB):** Easy, moderate capacity, good for small-medium tanks
- **Canister:** High capacity, customizable media, good for large tanks
- **Sponge:** Gentle, great for fry and shrimp, good biological filtration
- **Sump:** Highest capacity, hides equipment, for large/marine tanks
- **Internal:** Space-saving, moderate capacity

### The Golden Rule
NEVER replace all filter media at once. This destroys your beneficial bacteria colony and can crash your cycle.

## Planted Tanks

### Benefits of Live Plants
- Absorb nitrate (natural filtration)
- Provide hiding spots (reduces stress)
- Oxygenate water during light hours
- Compete with algae for nutrients
- Look beautiful

### Common Beginner Plants
- **Java Fern:** Low light, attach to wood/rock, nearly indestructible
- **Anubias:** Low light, slow growing, attach to hardscape
- **Java Moss:** Low light, versatile, great for fry hiding
- **Amazon Sword:** Medium light, root feeder, can get large
- **Hornwort:** Floating or planted, fast grower, nutrient sponge

## Algae

### Types and Causes
- **Green algae (glass):** Too much light — reduce photoperiod
- **Brown algae (diatoms):** New tank, low light — usually resolves on its own
- **Blue-green algae (cyanobacteria):** Low flow, high nutrients — actually bacteria, not algae
- **Black beard algae (BBA):** CO2 fluctuations, often on hardscape and slow-growing plants
- **Hair algae:** Excess nutrients and light, often in new planted setups
