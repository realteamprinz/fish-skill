# Fish Behavior Patterns Reference

## Normal Behaviors

### Swimming Patterns
- **Active, exploring:** Healthy, comfortable
- **Schooling tight:** Mild stress or new environment — can be normal in new fish
- **Hovering in one spot:** Resting (some species), or sick if prolonged
- **Surface gulping:** Low oxygen, or labyrinth breathers (bettas, gouramis) — normal for them
- **Glass surfing:** Seeing reflection, stressed, or new environment

### Feeding Behaviors
- **Rushing to food:** Healthy appetite, dominant fish
- **Picking at substrate:** Foraging — normal for bottom dwellers
- **Spitting out food:** Food too large, or picky eater, or gill irritation
- **Ignoring food:** New fish acclimating, or sick, or wrong food type

### Social Behaviors
- **Chasing:** Territorial, breeding, or aggression — context matters
- **Displaying (fin flaring):** Territorial or breeding display
- **Lip-locking:** Aggression (cichlids), establishing dominance
- **Parallel swimming:** Sizing each other up, potential fight or courtship

## Stress Indicators

### Physical Signs
- Clamped fins (fins held tight against body)
- Color fading or darkening
- Rapid gill movement
- Loss of appetite
- Hiding more than usual
- Sitting on bottom
- Flashing (rubbing against objects) — also sign of parasites

### Behavioral Signs
- Schooling fish separating from group
- Normally active fish becoming lethargic
- Normally peaceful fish becoming aggressive
- Changes in swimming level (bottom dweller going to surface)
- Gasping at surface

## Breeding Behaviors

### General Signs
- Color intensification (especially males)
- Nest building (bubble nests for bettas/gouramis, pit digging for cichlids)
- Increased territorial aggression
- Courtship displays (shimmying, dancing, chasing)
- Female developing rounder belly (eggs)
- Mouth-brooding (holding eggs/fry in mouth — cichlids)

## Disease Indicators

### Visual Symptoms to Track
| Symptom | Possible Cause |
|---|---|
| White spots on body | Ich (Ichthyophthirius) |
| Gold dust appearance | Velvet (Oodinium) |
| Cotton-like growths | Fungal infection |
| Red/inflamed gills | Ammonia burn or gill flukes |
| Raised scales (pineconing) | Dropsy — often fatal |
| Popeye (bulging eyes) | Bacterial infection or water quality |
| White stringy poop | Internal parasites |
| Fin edges turning white/ragged | Fin rot (bacterial) |
| Curved spine | Fish TB or genetic (livebearers) |
